We are neither beholden to the sun nor the moon.
We walk when we are rested, and we rest until we are ready to walk.

By day we see the pieces of a shattered place.
We do not pick them up.
We step over them, and we name them.

We hear the Grand Mother's words, whispered and kissed into every footprint.
"May sand and ash and fallen leaves
dissolve these capsized ships back from whence they came.
The program has run its course,
the prescription, filled. 
All is as it ever would be"

Our weary feet compact the sand into glass,
and by night trace glints and glimmers.
We pay the dunes tribute in the form of precious tears.
We gaze through the glass,
downwards and backwards in time.
We count the names now rearranged.
And brush them off our rosters.

The sky mother receives our goodbyes, our farewells,
and in the morning the earth mother receives our ashes.

Far away great oceans are formed
And from them great mountains rise, as if lifted by a string.
And upon them great rivers give way,
And from their waters, towering and strange plants grow.
The birds flock there in the cold times,
and return with beaks full of seeds.

If you ask, and if they hear you, 
they may drop one or two into your ear while you sleep.
This is how dreams are made.
The rest are sown into the land every time a bird sings.
